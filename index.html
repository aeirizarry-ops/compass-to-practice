Leadership Compass model

Four bearings that turn pressure into practice.

The Leadership Compass gives leaders a simple way to check direction before pressure takes over. Humility, Clarity, Courage, and Empathy help teams surface truth, align around priorities, and protect standards without losing people.

North

Humility

A humble leader listens before judging, asks what they may be missing, thanks people for honest input, and changes direction when the facts call for it.

East

Clarity

Say what changed, what matters, and what “done” means.

South

Courage

Name the barrier early. Protect the standard under pressure.

West

Empathy

Ask how the work is landing. Make truth safe enough to surface.

Center

Alignment

Product, process, and people reinforce each other: purpose is clear, constraints are visible, and ownership is shared.

About the author

Antonio Irizarry

Antonio created Compass To Practice to help leaders close the gap between what they intend to do and what people experience in the real world. His work centers on practical leadership, operational discipline, and the everyday behaviors that shape quality, trust, and performance.

Through clear stories, grounded reflection, and direct lessons from the field, Antonio invites listeners to examine how they lead under pressure and how small, consistent practices can create lasting impact.
